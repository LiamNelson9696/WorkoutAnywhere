SMMSeated Shoulder Press
Adjust the height of the Smith machine bar to a position just above your head while seated on a bench with back support. Sit on the bench with your feet firmly planted on the ground and your back flat against the bench. Grasp the bar with a shoulder-width grip and your palms facing forward. With your elbows slightly below your shoulders and the bar at shoulder height, press the bar up until your arms are fully extended. Hold the bar at the top of the movement for a moment, and then lower the bar back down to the starting position, keeping your elbows slightly below your shoulders.
CMMCable Shrugs
Start by standing in front of a cable machine with the handle attached to the low pulley. Grab the handle with both hands, palms facing inwards, and step back to create tension on the cable. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent. Keep your arms straight and your elbows slightly bent throughout the exercise. Slowly raise your shoulders towards your ears in a shrugging motion, making sure to keep your arms straight and your back straight. Hold the contracted position for a second and then slowly lower your shoulders back down to the starting position.
CMMFace Pulls
Set the cable machine at a height so that it is level with your face. Attach a rope handle to the cable machine. Stand facing the cable machine and grab the rope handle with both hands, palms facing each other. Take a step back to create some tension in the cable and lean back slightly. Start the movement by pulling the rope handle towards your face while keeping your elbows high and out to the sides. When you pull the rope towards your face, try to focus on squeezing your shoulder blades together. Once the rope handle reaches your face, hold the contraction for a second or two. Slowly return the rope handle to the starting position while keeping the tension in the cable.
SPMShoulder Press Machine
Adjust the seat height so that the handles of the machine are at shoulder level. Sit down on the seat and position yourself with your back flat against the backrest. Grasp the handles with an overhand grip and make sure your elbows are directly beneath your wrists. Press the handles upwards by straightening your arms until they are fully extended, but not locked out. Hold for a moment at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the handles back down to shoulder level.
LRMLateral Raise Machine
Adjust the seat and the height of the handles of the machine to fit your body. Sit on the seat with your back straight and your feet firmly planted on the floor. Place your elbows on the padded arms of the machine and grip the handles with your palms facing down. Keep your back straight, engage your core muscles, and exhale as you raise your arms out to your sides to shoulder height. Keep your elbows slightly bent throughout the movement. Hold the contraction for a second and then inhale as you lower your arms back down to the starting position.
DBDumbbell Overhead Shoulder Press
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder level with your palms facing forward and your elbows bent. Brace your core and engage your shoulder blades, keeping your back straight. Press the dumbbells up overhead, extending your arms fully and bringing the weights together at the top. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to shoulder level, keeping your elbows tucked in and maintaining control throughout the movement.
BBBarbell Overhead Shoulder Press
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and grip a barbell with both hands using a slightly wider than shoulder-width grip. Rest the barbell on your upper chest with your elbows bent and pointed forward. Brace your core and engage your shoulder blades, keeping your back straight. Press the barbell up overhead, extending your arms fully and bringing the barbell over the top of your head. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the barbell back down to your upper chest, keeping your elbows tucked in and maintaining control throughout the movement.
DBDumbbell Front Delt Raise
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a pair of dumbbells at your sides with your palms facing your thighs. Brace your core and engage your shoulder blades, keeping your back straight. Slowly lift the dumbbells up in front of your body, keeping your elbows slightly bent and your palms facing downward. Continue lifting the dumbbells until they reach shoulder level. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to your sides, maintaining control throughout the movement.
KBKettlebell Front Delt Raise
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a kettlebell with one hand at your side with your palm facing your thigh. Brace your core and engage your shoulder blades, keeping your back straight. Slowly lift the kettlebell up in front of your body, keeping your elbow slightly bent and your palm facing downward. Continue lifting the kettlebell until it reaches shoulder level. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the kettlebell back down to your side, maintaining control throughout the movement.
DBDumbbell Lateral Delt Raise
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a pair of dumbbells at your sides with your palms facing your thighs. Brace your core and engage your shoulder blades, keeping your back straight. Keeping your elbows slightly bent, slowly lift the dumbbells out to the sides, keeping your palms facing downward. Continue lifting the dumbbells until they reach shoulder level, being careful not to lift them higher than your shoulders. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to your sides, maintaining control throughout the movement.

Keeping your elbow slightly bent, slowly lift the kettlebell out to the side, keeping your palm facing downward. DBBent-Over Reverse Fly
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a pair of dumbbells with an overhand grip. Hinge at the hips and bend forward, keeping your back straight, until your upper body is parallel to the ground. Keep a slight bend in your elbows and lift the dumbbells out to the sides, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Continue lifting the dumbbells until they are in line with your shoulders. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position, maintaining control throughout the movement.
DBArnold Press
Start by sitting on an exercise bench or chair with a pair of dumbbells in your hands, palms facing your body. Raise the dumbbells to your shoulder level with your palms facing your body, and your elbows at a 90-degree angle. While keeping your palms facing your body, slowly press the dumbbells up above your head. As you press the dumbbells up, rotate your palms until they are facing away from your body. At the top of the movement, your arms should be fully extended with your palms facing forward. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to shoulder level while simultaneously rotating your palms back towards your body. Lower the dumbbells to the starting position with your palms facing your body and your elbows at a 90-degree angle.
DBDumbbell Upright Row
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a pair of dumbbells with an overhand grip in front of your thighs. Keep the dumbbells close to your body and raise them towards your chin, keeping your elbows pointed out to the sides. Pause for a moment at the top of the movement, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position with control.
BBBarbell Upright Row
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an overhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart. Allow the barbell to hang in front of your thighs with your arms fully extended and your palms facing your body. Keeping your elbows pointed out to the sides, lift the barbell towards your chin, keeping it close to your body. Pause for a moment at the top of the movement, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Lower the barbell back down to the starting position with control.
DBCircle Press
Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and holding a pair of dumbbells at shoulder height with your palms facing forward. Press the dumbbells overhead until your arms are fully extended, with your palms facing forward. Lower the dumbbells down to your chest level while rotating your palms to face each other. Lower the dumbbells further down to your sides while rotating your palms to face backward. Bring the dumbbells back to the starting position while rotating your palms to face forward again.
