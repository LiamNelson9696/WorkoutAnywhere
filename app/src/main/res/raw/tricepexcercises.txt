CMMTricep Pushdowns
Set the cable machine to a high pulley, and attach a straight or V-shaped bar to the cable. Stand facing the machine with your feet shoulder-width apart, and grab the bar with an overhand grip, with your hands about shoulder-width apart. Bring your elbows close to your sides, and make sure your upper arms are parallel to the ground. This is your starting position. Keeping your elbows stationary, exhale and extend your arms down until your arms are fully extended. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement to contract your triceps. Inhale and slowly return to the starting position, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
BBBarbell Skullcrusher
Lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the ground, and grasp a barbell with an overhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart. Hold the barbell with your arms extended straight up towards the ceiling, with your palms facing forward. Slowly lower the barbell towards your forehead, keeping your elbows pointed straight up towards the ceiling and close to your head. Pause for a moment at the bottom of the movement, with the barbell hovering just above your forehead. Engage your triceps to lift the barbell back up to the starting position.
DBDumbbell Skullcrusher
Lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the ground, and hold a pair of dumbbells with an overhand grip, palms facing each other. Hold the dumbbells with your arms extended straight up towards the ceiling, with your palms facing each other. Slowly lower the dumbbells towards your temples, keeping your elbows pointed straight up towards the ceiling and close to your head. Pause for a moment at the bottom of the movement, with the dumbbells hovering just above your temples. Engage your triceps to lift the dumbbells back up to the starting position.
BBClose-Grip Bench Press
Lie flat on a bench, and grasp the barbell with a close grip, with your hands shoulder-width apart or slightly narrower. Your palms should be facing away from you. Lift the barbell off the rack and hold it straight above your chest with your arms fully extended. Slowly lower the barbell towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body. The bar should touch your chest or come very close to it. Pause for a moment at the bottom of the movement, then push the barbell back up to the starting position.
DBDumbbell Overhead Triceps Extension
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell with both hands. Lift the dumbbell over your head, fully extending your arms with your palms facing each other. Your elbows should be close to your ears. Keeping your upper arms still, bend your elbows to lower the dumbbell behind your head. Your forearms should be parallel to the floor and your elbows should be pointing straight up. Pause for a moment at the bottom of the movement, then straighten your elbows to raise the dumbbell back up to the starting position.
BBSingle-Arm Landmine Press
Set up a landmine station by inserting one end of a barbell into a landmine attachment or placing the barbell in a corner. Load weight plates onto the opposite end of the barbell. Stand facing the barbell with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent. Grab the end of the barbell with one hand and bring it to your shoulder, with your elbow bent and pointing down. Tighten your core and glutes, and then push the barbell away from your body, extending your arm and pressing the weight up and away from your body. Keep your chest up and your shoulder blades down and back throughout the movement. Pause at the top of the movement for a moment, then slowly lower the weight back down to your starting position.
DBDumbbell Kickbacks
Hold a dumbbell in one hand and stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and your core engaged. Bend your elbow to bring the dumbbell close to your side, keeping your upper arm parallel to the ground. Your forearm should be perpendicular to the ground. Keeping your upper arm stationary, extend your forearm back until your arm is straight, squeezing your triceps at the top of the movement. Pause for a moment at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weight back down to your starting position.
KBKettlebell Push Press
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a kettlebell in one hand at shoulder height. The kettlebell should be held in a rack position, with your elbow tucked in and your wrist straight. Bend your knees slightly and then quickly straighten your legs, using the momentum to help you push the kettlebell overhead. At the same time, push the kettlebell up with your arm. Lock out your arm at the top of the movement, with your elbow fully extended and the kettlebell directly overhead. Lower the kettlebell back down to the starting position by bending your elbow and lowering the kettlebell back down to your shoulder.
KBKettlebell Tall Kneeling Press
Start in a tall kneeling position with your knees hip-width apart and your toes tucked under. Hold a kettlebell in one hand at shoulder height with your elbow tucked in and your wrist straight. Engage your core and keep your body upright as you press the kettlebell overhead. As you press, make sure to keep your elbow tucked in and your wrist straight. Once the kettlebell is fully extended overhead, pause for a second, then slowly lower the kettlebell back down to the starting position at your shoulder.
KBKettlebell Lunge and Press
Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a kettlebell in your right hand at your shoulder with your elbow tucked in. Step forward with your left foot into a lunge position, keeping your back straight and your right foot firmly planted on the ground. As you lunge down, press the kettlebell overhead with your right arm, extending your arm fully at the top of the movement. Lower the kettlebell back down to your shoulder as you stand up out of the lunge.
