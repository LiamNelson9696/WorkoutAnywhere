MRMMachine Rows
Adjust the weight and seat height on the machine to your preference. Sit on the machine with your chest against the pad and your feet on the platform. Reach forward and grab the handles with an overhand grip, keeping your arms straight. Pull the handles towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together and keeping your elbows close to your sides. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then slowly release back to the starting position.
LPMLat Pulldown
Begin by sitting on the lat pulldown machine with your knees securely under the knee pad. Adjust the weight according to your fitness level and grasp the bar with an overhand grip that is slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Keeping your core engaged and your back straight, pull the bar down towards your chest. Your elbows should be pointing down towards the floor. Pause for a moment and then slowly release the bar back up to the starting position, making sure to keep your back straight and your shoulder blades down.
SMMSmith Machine Rows
Adjust the Smith Machine bar to a height where you can reach it comfortably from a standing position. Stand facing the machine with your feet shoulder-width apart, and grasp the bar with an overhand grip with your hands slightly closer than shoulder-width apart. Keep your back straight and your core engaged, and slowly pull the bar towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together as you do so. Pause for a moment at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the bar back to the starting position.
BBDeadlift
Approach the barbell and stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, with the barbell directly over the center of your feet. The barbell should be close to your shins, and your toes should be pointing forward. Squat down and grasp the barbell with an overhand grip, with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Your palms should be facing your body. Straighten your legs and lift the barbell off the ground, keeping your back flat and your core tight. The barbell should stay close to your shins as you lift it. Once the barbell is past your knees, use the power from your legs to drive your hips forward and stand up straight. At the top of the movement, pause briefly and squeeze your glutes and core. Lower the barbell back down to the ground in a controlled manner, keeping your back flat and your core tight. Once the barbell is back on the ground, reset your position and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
BBBent-Over Row
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent. Hold a barbell with an overhand grip, your hands shoulder-width apart. Lean forward at your hips, keeping your back straight, until your torso is nearly parallel to the floor. Pull the barbell towards your lower chest by bending your elbows and keeping them close to your body. Keep your back straight throughout the movement and squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement. Lower the barbell back down to the starting position with control.
DBSingle-Arm Row
Place a dumbbell on the floor next to a bench. Stand next to the bench and place your hand on the bench, keeping your back flat and your feet shoulder-width apart. Pick up the dumbbell with your other hand, keeping your palm facing your body and your back straight. Lift the dumbbell towards your torso by bending your elbow and keeping it close to your body. Squeeze your shoulder blade at the top of the movement. Lower the dumbbell back down to the starting position with control.
BBLandmine row
Stand facing a barbell that's inserted into a corner of a wall. Load weights onto the end of the barbell. Straddle the barbell with your feet shoulder-width apart and hinge at the hips, keeping your back straight and your chest up. With one hand, grip the end of the barbell closest to you, using an overhand grip. Lift the barbell towards your torso by bending your elbow and keeping it close to your body. Squeeze your shoulder blade at the top of the movement. Lower the barbell back down to the starting position with control.
KBFarmer's Carry
Stand tall with your feet shoulder-width apart and a kettlebell beside each foot. Hinge at the hips and reach down to grab the handles of both kettlebells. Engage your core and lats, and lift the kettlebells off the ground by standing up. Keep your shoulders down and back, and maintain a neutral spine and a tight grip on the kettlebells. Walk forward with small, quick steps, keeping your arms straight by your sides and your shoulders stabilized. Focus on keeping your posture tall and your steps steady, and try to keep the kettlebells from swinging or touching your body. Continue to walk forward for a set distance or time, then lower the kettlebells back to the ground by hinging at the hips and placing them gently down.

DBKroc Row
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in one hand with an overhand grip. Hinge forward at the hips, bending your knees slightly and keeping your back straight. Your torso should be almost parallel to the floor. Let the dumbbell hang straight down toward the floor, with your arm fully extended. Pull the dumbbell up toward your chest, keeping your elbow close to your body and your shoulder blades squeezed together. Once the dumbbell reaches your chest, pause for a second and then slowly lower it back down to the starting position.
BBPendlay Row
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and grip the barbell with an overhand grip that is slightly wider than shoulder-width. Bend your knees and hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and your head in a neutral position. Your torso should be at about a 45-degree angle to the ground. Pull the barbell up toward your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body and squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement. Lower the barbell back down to the starting position, making sure to maintain proper form throughout the exercise.
BBMeadows Row
Place a loaded barbell on the floor and straddle it, with your feet shoulder-width apart. Bend your knees slightly and hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back flat and your core engaged. Grab the barbell with one hand using an overhand grip, with your palm facing towards your body. Your hand should be just outside your knee. Lift the barbell off the floor by driving your elbow back and up towards your hip, keeping it close to your body. Your upper arm should be parallel to the floor. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, squeezing your back muscles, before lowering the weight back down to the starting position. Complete the desired number of repetitions on one side before switching to the other side.
BBPower Clean
Start with the barbell on the floor in front of you. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, with your toes pointing slightly outwards. The barbell should be positioned over the middle of your feet. Squat down and grip the barbell with your hands, just outside your shins. Your hands should be shoulder-width apart with your palms facing down. Take a deep breath and brace your core. Explosively extend your hips, knees, and ankles, driving the barbell upwards. Keep your arms straight during this phase and keep the bar close to your body. Once the bar reaches your mid-thigh, quickly shrug your shoulders and pull your elbows high and to the outside. This will cause the bar to continue to rise and will bring it up to your shoulders. Catch the barbell at your shoulders, with your elbows pointing forward. Your hips should be fully extended, and your knees should be slightly bent. Stand up straight with the barbell at your shoulders. Take a deep breath and brace your core. Lower the barbell to the starting position on the floor, and repeat for the desired number of repetitions. Note: This is a complex exercise that requires proper technique and form to perform safely and effectively. It is recommended to seek guidance from a qualified coach or trainer if you are new to this exercise.
