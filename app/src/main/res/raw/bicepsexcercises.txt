BBStanding Barbell Curl
Stand straight with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent. Hold the barbell with an underhand grip, keeping your hands shoulder-width apart. Keeping your elbows close to your sides and your upper arms stationary, exhale and slowly curl the barbell up towards your chest. Pause for a moment when the barbell is at the top of the movement, then inhale and slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position.
BBReverse Barbell Curl
Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart and grasp a barbell with an overhand grip, with your hands shoulder-width apart or slightly closer. Hold the barbell in front of your thighs, with your arms extended straight down and your palms facing down. While keeping your elbows close to your sides, exhale and slowly curl the barbell up towards your shoulders, contracting your biceps. Pause for a second at the top of the movement, squeezing your biceps, then inhale and slowly lower the barbell back down to the starting position.
BBProne Incline Barbell Curl
Adjust the incline bench to a 45-degree angle and lie face down on it with your chest against the bench and your feet flat on the floor. Grasp the barbell with an underhand grip, with your hands shoulder-width apart. Allow your arms to fully extend towards the floor, keeping your elbows tucked into your sides. Keeping your elbows stationary, curl the barbell towards your chest by contracting your biceps. Squeeze your biceps at the top of the curl and then lower the barbell back down to the starting position with control.
BBBarbell Drag Curl
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an underhand grip, keeping your hands shoulder-width apart. Start with the barbell resting on your thighs, and keep your elbows close to your body. Curl the bar up towards your chest, but instead of keeping your elbows stationary, drag the bar up your body, keeping it in contact with your torso. Squeeze your biceps at the top of the movement and then slowly lower the bar back down to the starting position.
BBBarbell Bent-Over Row
Begin by standing in front of a barbell with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing forward. The barbell should be resting on the ground in front of you. Bend forward at the hips while keeping your back straight and your chest up. You should be looking forward at a point on the ground a few feet in front of you. Grasp the barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Inhale and engage your core as you begin to pull the barbell up towards your chest, bending your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together. Keep your arms close to your sides and your elbows pointing behind you. Pause at the top of the movement, then exhale as you slowly lower the barbell back down to the starting position.
BBBarbell Preacher Curl
Adjust the preacher bench to the appropriate height and position it so that you can comfortably sit on it with your armpits resting on the top of the pad and your arms fully extended over the pad. Grasp the barbell with an underhand grip that is slightly narrower than shoulder-width apart. Your palms should be facing up. Keeping your upper arms and elbows in a fixed position, curl the barbell up towards your shoulders while exhaling. Pause for a moment at the top of the movement, squeezing your biceps. Lower the barbell back down to the starting position while inhaling.
DBSupinated Biceps Curl
Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart and your arms hanging by your sides. Hold a pair of dumbbells with your palms facing upward. Keep your elbows close to your sides and slowly raise the dumbbells towards your shoulders while exhaling. As you lift, keep your upper arms stationary and only move your forearms. When you reach the top of the movement, squeeze your biceps for a second, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position while inhaling.
DBDumbbell Reverse Bicep Curl
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing down. Keep your elbows close to your body and your upper arms stationary as you curl the dumbbells towards your shoulders. Keep your palms facing down throughout the movement and avoid rotating your wrists. Pause at the top of the movement and squeeze your biceps and forearms. Slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position with control.
DBDumbbell Hammer Curl
Stand up straight with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing your thighs. Keep your elbows close to your sides and slowly curl the weights towards your shoulders. As you lift the weights, keep your palms facing your body the entire time (like you're holding a hammer). Squeeze your biceps at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
DBZottman Bicep Curl
Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand, with your palms facing forward and your arms fully extended down by your sides. Curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your body and your palms facing up. Once the dumbbells are at shoulder level, rotate your wrists so that your palms are facing down, and hold this position for a second. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position, with your palms facing down the entire time. At the bottom, rotate your wrists back so that your palms are facing forward, and repeat the exercise for the desired number of repetitions.
DBConcentration Bicep Curl
Sit on a bench or chair with your feet firmly planted on the ground. Take a dumbbell in your right hand and place your right elbow on the inside of your right thigh, with your arm fully extended and the weight hanging down. Keep your back straight and your core engaged. Curl the dumbbell towards your shoulder by bending your elbow while keeping your upper arm stationary. Squeeze your bicep at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weight back down to the starting position.
DBDumbbell Incline Bicep Curl
Set an incline bench to a 45-degree angle and sit on it with your back straight and your feet planted firmly on the floor. Hold a pair of dumbbells with an underhand grip and let your arms hang straight down at your sides. Your palms should be facing forward. Keeping your upper arms stationary, curl the weights toward your shoulders, exhaling as you lift the weights. Focus on contracting your biceps as you curl. Pause at the top of the movement and squeeze your biceps, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower.
KBStanding Kettlebell Bicep Curl
Stand straight with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a kettlebell in each hand with your palms facing forward. Keep your elbows close to your sides and your upper arms stationary. Slowly curl the kettlebells towards your shoulders, exhaling as you lift them up. Keep your wrists straight and contract your biceps at the top of the movement. Pause for a second, and then slowly lower the kettlebells back down to the starting position, inhaling as you lower them.
KBIncline Kettlebell Curl
Set up an incline bench at about a 45-degree angle and place a kettlebell at the bottom of the bench. Sit down on the bench and lean back against it with your arms straight, holding the kettlebell in an underhand grip with your palms facing up. Slowly curl the kettlebell up towards your shoulder while keeping your elbows tucked in close to your sides. Keep your wrists straight and don't let the weight swing or use momentum to lift it. Pause for a second at the top of the movement and then slowly lower the kettlebell back down to the starting position.
KBKettlebell Hammer Curl
Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart, and hold a kettlebell in each hand by your sides with your palms facing each other. Keep your back straight and your core engaged throughout the movement. Slowly curl the kettlebells up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows tucked in close to your body. As you curl the weights, focus on squeezing your bicep and forearm muscles. Once you have curled the kettlebells up as high as you can, hold the position for a second or two, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
KBReverse Bottoms-Up Bicep Curl
Hold a kettlebell with the bottom up grip. The kettlebell should rest on the handle with the ball at the top. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your core engaged. Keep your elbows close to your body and your palms facing down. Begin the curl by raising the kettlebell towards your shoulder, making sure to keep your wrist straight and your arm close to your body. Pause for a moment when the kettlebell is at shoulder level. Lower the kettlebell back down to the starting position in a controlled manner.
