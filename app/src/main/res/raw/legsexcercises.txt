LEMLeg Extensions
Adjust the machine: Sit on the leg extension machine and adjust the backrest and footpad so that your knees align with the machine's axis of rotation, and your feet are placed flat on the footpad. Set the weight: Select the desired weight and lock it into place on the machine. Perform the exercise: Slowly raise your feet up and out in front of you, straightening your legs as you lift. At the top of the movement, pause briefly and contract your quadriceps muscles, then slowly lower the weight back down to the starting position.
SMMSmith Machine Back Squat
Start by placing the barbell on the rack at a height that allows you to comfortably unrack it. Stand under the bar with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing slightly outward. Position yourself under the bar so that it rests across your upper traps, just below your neck. Grasp the bar with a slightly wider than shoulder-width grip and lift it off the rack. Take a step or two back from the rack and position your feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider. Your toes should be pointing slightly outward. Take a deep breath and brace your core. This will help you maintain proper spinal alignment throughout the movement. Begin the descent by pushing your hips back and bending your knees. Keep your weight on your heels and maintain a tall chest. Continue descending until your thighs are parallel to the ground or just below parallel. Your knees should be directly above your toes and your shins should be vertical. Pause briefly at the bottom of the squat, then begin ascending by driving your heels into the ground and extending your hips and knees. As you rise, keep your chest tall and your core braced. Continue to drive through your heels until you reach the starting position.
SMMSmith Machine Romanian Dead Lift
Stand in front of the Smith machine with your feet shoulder-width apart. Place the bar at hip level and load it with the desired weight. Step under the bar and position it against your upper thighs. Grasp the bar with an overhand grip and straighten your legs. Keeping your back straight and your core tight, bend at the hips and lower the bar toward the floor. Continue to lower the bar until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings. Pause briefly and then raise the bar back up by pushing your hips forward and extending your legs.
SMMSmith Machine Front Squat
Adjust the bar height: Start by adjusting the bar height so that it is at the level of your upper chest. Stand under the bar: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointed slightly outward. Position yourself under the bar and grip it with your palms facing up. Keep your elbows up and your upper arms parallel to the ground. Lift the bar off the rack: Stand up to lift the bar off the rack, take a step back, and position your feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Make sure your feet are firmly planted on the ground. Lower into a squat: Begin the squat by bending at the knees and hips simultaneously, keeping your back straight and chest up. Lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground. Drive back up: Push through your heels and drive your hips forward to return to the starting position.
SMMSmith Machine Hack Squat
Stand facing the Smith Machine with your feet hip-width apart and your toes pointing forward. Your shoulders should be directly under the bar. Reach up and grasp the bar with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Unrack the bar and step back to clear the rack. Keeping your chest up and your core engaged, slowly lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the floor. Pause briefly at the bottom, then push through your heels and extend your legs to return to the starting position.
LCMLeg Curl Machine
Adjust the machine: First, adjust the machine so that the leg pad is positioned comfortably against the back of your lower legs. Your knees should be just off the edge of the seat. Lie down on the machine: Lie face down on the machine's bench with your knees just off the edge of the seat. Position your legs under the padded lever, which should be just above your heels. Grasp the handles: Reach forward and grasp the handles provided to stabilize yourself on the bench. Curl your legs: Keeping your hips down on the bench, exhale and contract your hamstrings to raise the padded lever as far as you can toward your glutes. Hold for a second and then inhale as you lower the padded lever back to the starting position.
LLMLeg Press Machine
Adjust the seat: Sit on the leg press machine and adjust the seat so that your feet can comfortably rest on the platform with your knees bent at a 90-degree angle. Adjust the weight: Choose the weight you want to lift by adjusting the weight plates on the machine. Make sure you start with a lighter weight and gradually increase as you get comfortable. Position your feet: Place your feet shoulder-width apart on the platform with your toes pointed slightly outward. Grip the handles: Grasp the handles located on either side of the seat to stabilize your body. Push the platform: Slowly push the platform away from your body until your legs are almost fully extended but not locked out. Bring the platform back: Slowly bend your knees and bring the platform back towards your body until your knees are at a 90-degree angle.
CMMCable Leg Extensions
Adjust the weight of the cable machine to a suitable resistance and attach the ankle cuff to the cable. Stand with your left side facing the cable machine and grasp the machine with your right hand for support. Place your left leg through the ankle cuff and secure it firmly around your ankle. Slowly extend your left leg forward until it is fully extended, then pause for a moment at the end of the movement to contract your quadriceps muscles. Slowly bring your left leg back to the starting position while maintaining control over the movement. Complete the desired number of repetitions, then switch sides and repeat the exercise with your right leg.
CMMCable Pull Through
Start by attaching a rope to a low pulley cable machine and setting the weight to a comfortable level. Stand facing away from the machine, with your feet shoulder-width apart and your back facing the weight stack. Hold the rope with both hands between your legs, with your palms facing each other. Hinge at your hips and push your buttocks back while maintaining a slight bend in your knees. Keep your chest up and your back straight. Pull the rope back and up through your legs as you straighten your hips and stand up, squeezing your glutes at the top of the movement. Lower the rope back down through your legs and return to the starting position with a controlled movement.
BBBarbell Deadlift
Approach the bar and stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. The bar should be directly above the middle of your feet, with your toes pointing forward or slightly outwards. Bend down and grip the bar with your palms facing down, your hands shoulder-width apart, and your fingers wrapped around the bar. Lower your hips down until your shins touch the bar. Keep your back flat, your head facing forward, and your chest up. Take a deep breath, brace your core, and begin lifting the bar by straightening your legs. As the bar passes your knees, push your hips forward until you reach a standing position. At the top of the movement, squeeze your glutes and brace your core. Lower the bar back down to the ground by reversing the movement. Ensure that you maintain proper form throughout the exercise, keeping your back straight, your head facing forward, and your chest up.
BBBarbell Good Morning
Begin by loading a barbell with weights and standing with your feet shoulder-width apart. The bar should be resting on your traps, just below your neck, and your hands should be gripping it slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Keeping your back straight and your core engaged, hinge forward at the hips, pushing your glutes back as you lower the bar towards the floor. Keep your knees slightly bent throughout the movement and avoid rounding your back. Lower the bar as far as you can while maintaining good form and feeling a stretch in your hamstrings. Reverse the movement by pushing your hips forward and standing up tall, squeezing your glutes at the top of the movement.
BBBarbellSplit Stance Lunge
Stand with your feet hip-width apart, with a barbell resting on your shoulders behind your neck. Take a large step forward with your right foot, placing it about two feet in front of your left foot. Keep your torso upright and engage your core to maintain stability. Lower your body by bending both knees until your left knee almost touches the ground. Make sure your right knee stays directly above your ankle, and your left knee points toward the ground. Pause for a second at the bottom, then push off your right foot to return to the starting position. Repeat on the other side, taking a large step forward with your left foot and lowering your body until your right knee almost touches the ground. Continue alternating sides for the desired number of repetitions.
BBBarebell Hip Thrust
Sit on the ground with your back against a bench or step, and place a barbell across your hips. Roll the barbell towards your hips so that it rests on your lap. Make sure the barbell is secure and stable. Place your feet flat on the ground with your knees bent, and your toes pointing straight ahead. Brace your core, squeeze your glutes, and lift your hips up towards the ceiling until your body is in a straight line from your shoulders to your knees. Pause for a second at the top of the movement, then slowly lower your hips back down to the starting position.
BBBarbell Landmine Deadlift
Start by placing one end of the barbell into a corner of the room. Load the other end of the barbell with weight plates. Stand facing the landmine attachment with your feet shoulder-width apart. Squat down and grab the barbell with both hands, keeping your back straight and your core tight. Lift the barbell up off the ground, driving through your heels and using your glutes, hamstrings, and lower back muscles. Keep your chest up and your shoulders back throughout the movement. Lower the barbell back down to the ground with control, maintaining proper form.
DBDumbbell Bulgarian Split Squat
Stand in front of a bench or sturdy elevated platform with a dumbbell in each hand. Step your left foot back and place the top of your foot on the bench. Position your right foot forward, keeping your knee directly above your ankle. Engage your core and keep your torso upright as you lower your body down into a lunge position. Your right knee should be at a 90-degree angle. Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement before pressing through your right heel to return to the starting position. Repeat the movement for the desired number of reps before switching sides and performing the exercise with your left leg.
DBDumbbell Romanian Deadlift
Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at arm's length in front of your thighs, with your palms facing your thighs. Engage your core and maintain a slight arch in your lower back throughout the exercise. Take a step backward with your left foot, keeping your right foot firmly on the ground. Slowly hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and your chest lifted, and lower the dumbbells towards the ground until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings. Once you feel the stretch, pause for a moment, and then return to the starting position by squeezing your glutes and pushing your hips forward. Repeat the movement for the desired number of repetitions and then switch sides by stepping back with your right foot.
DBDumbbell Single-Leg RDL
Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold a pair of dumbbells in front of your thighs, with your palms facing your body. Shift your weight onto your left foot and lift your right foot off the ground slightly. Keep your left knee slightly bent. Hinge forward at your hips, lowering your torso and lifting your right leg behind you. Keep your back flat and your core engaged. Lower the dumbbells towards the ground, keeping them close to your legs. Lower until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings. Pause for a moment, then use your hamstrings and glutes to lift your torso back up to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch sides and repeat the exercise with your right foot on the ground and your left foot lifted.
DBDumbbell Goblet Squat
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell vertically by one end against your chest with both hands. Your elbows should be pointing down, and your palms should be facing up. Engage your core and keep your chest up as you lower your hips back and down as if you are sitting into a chair. Make sure your knees track over your toes and don't cave inwards. Keep your weight on your heels and go down as low as you can while maintaining proper form. Your thighs should be parallel to the floor or lower. Pause at the bottom of the squat, then drive through your heels to stand up straight, squeezing your glutes at the top.
DBDumbbell Hip Thrust
Place a bench or box on the floor, and position a pair of dumbbells on either side of it. Sit on the ground with your back to the bench or box, and rest your upper back against its edge. Place your feet flat on the ground, shoulder-width apart, and position the dumbbells on your hips. Engage your core and glutes, and drive through your heels to lift your hips off the ground. Press the dumbbells against your hips throughout the movement to keep them in place. Lift your hips as high as possible, squeezing your glutes at the top of the movement. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then lower your hips back down to the ground in a controlled manner.
DBDumbbell Side Lunge
Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides. Take a big step to your right side with your right foot, keeping your left foot planted on the ground. Bend your right knee and push your hips back, lowering your body toward the ground. Keep your chest lifted and your back straight as you lower your body. Push through your right heel to stand up and return to the starting position. Repeat on the opposite side, stepping out with your left foot. Continue alternating sides for the desired number of repetitions.
KBKettlebell Swings
Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and your kettlebell on the floor in front of you. Hinge forward at the hips and grab the kettlebell handle with both hands, keeping your back flat and your arms straight. Engage your core and hike the kettlebell back between your legs, maintaining a slight bend in your knees. Explosively extend your hips, driving the kettlebell forward and up to shoulder height. Your arms should remain straight, and the power for the movement should come from your hips and glutes. As the kettlebell descends, hinge back at the hips and let it swing back between your legs, keeping your core tight and your back flat. Repeat the movement for the desired number of reps. It's important to note that the kettlebell swing is a high-intensity exercise that requires proper form and technique to avoid injury. It's recommended that you work with a qualified trainer or coach to learn the proper technique before attempting this exercise on your own.
KBSingle-Leg KettleBell Deadlift
Begin by standing with your feet hip-width apart and hold a kettlebell in your right hand. Lift your left foot off the ground and extend it backward so that it's straight and parallel to the floor. Hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and your core engaged, while lowering the kettlebell towards the ground. Keep your right knee slightly bent and your left foot flexed. Lower the kettlebell until you feel a stretch in your right hamstring and glute. Keep your arm straight as you lower the kettlebell. Pause for a moment at the bottom of the movement, then slowly return to the starting position by squeezing your glutes and extending your hips. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch to the other leg and repeat.
KBKettlebell Goblet Squat

Begin by bending your knees and hips, lowering yourself into a squat position. Make sure your knees are aligned with your toes and your weight is evenly distributed over your feet. Continue lowering until your hips are slightly below your knees, or until your thighs are parallel to the ground. Pause briefly in the bottom position, and then exhale as you press through your heels to return to the starting position.
KBKettlebell Bulgarian Split Squat
Stand in front of a bench or sturdy elevated surface with your back facing it. Hold a kettlebell in your right hand by the handle with an overhand grip and let your arm hang at your side. Place the top of your left foot on the bench behind you, with your laces facing down and your toes pointing forward. Your right foot should be far enough away from the bench so that when you lower into the squat, your right knee tracks directly over your right ankle. Brace your core and keep your chest up as you lower your body down by bending your right knee and hip. Keep your left leg straight and your left heel elevated off the bench. Continue lowering your body until your right thigh is parallel to the ground, or as low as you can comfortably go while maintaining good form. Pause at the bottom of the movement, then push through your right foot to stand back up to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps, then switch to the other side by holding the kettlebell in your left hand and placing your right foot on the bench. Note: It's important to keep your knee in line with your ankle, avoid letting it cave inward or outward. Also, ensure that your back stays straight and your chest stays up throughout the movement.
